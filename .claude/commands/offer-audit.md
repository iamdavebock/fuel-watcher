# Offer Audit

Run a Hormozi-style audit of an existing (or planned) offer, score it against the value equation, and produce `offer-audit.md` with a prioritised fix list. The Growth team executes from the findings.

---

## The Interview

Run in 4 sections. Ask each section's questions **all at once**, wait for the full response, then move on. No mid-section follow-ups.

### Section 1 — The Offer As It Stands

1. What exactly does the customer get? (deliverables, format, duration)
2. What does it cost, and how is it paid? (one-time / instalments / recurring)
3. Who is it for — describe the specific buyer, not the broad market.
4. What happens in the customer's first 7 days after buying?

### Section 2 — The Promise

1. What outcome does the customer actually want? (their words, not yours)
2. How long until they see the first real result?
3. What does the customer have to DO for it to work? (effort, time, behaviour change)
4. Why should they believe it will work for *them*? (proof: results, testimonials, mechanism)

### Section 3 — Risk & Friction

1. What guarantee, if any, exists today? Exact wording.
2. What are the 3 most common objections or reasons people don't buy?
3. What do prospects compare you against, and what do those alternatives cost?
4. Is there any genuine scarcity or urgency? (real capacity limits, deadlines)

### Section 4 — Economics

1. Roughly what does it cost you to deliver one unit/client?
2. What's the average customer worth over their lifetime?
3. What % of interested prospects buy? (best guess is fine)

---

## Scoring — The Value Equation

Score each lever 1–5 with one-line justification:

| Lever | Question | Score |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Dream outcome | Is the promised outcome the one the market actually craves? | /5 |
| Perceived likelihood | Would a sceptic believe it'll work for them? (proof density) | /5 |
| Time delay | How fast is the first meaningful win? | /5 |
| Effort & sacrifice | How much does the customer have to do? | /5 |
| Risk reversal | Does the guarantee remove the fear of buying? | /5 |
| Price-to-value framing | Is framed value ≥ 5× price? | /5 |

**Overall: /30.** Anything ≤3 is a finding.

---

## Delegation

After scoring, delegate fixes to the Growth team:

| Finding type | Agent |
|--------------|-------|
| Weak stack, pricing, guarantee, naming | `offer-architect` |
| No lead magnet / weak front-end | `leadgen` |
| Conversion or churn problem | `growth` |
| Positioning unclear / wrong avatar | `brand-strategist` |
| Sales calls not closing | `sales-closer` |

---

## Output — `offer-audit.md`

```
# Offer Audit — [offer name] ([date])

## Snapshot
[offer, price, avatar — 3 lines]

## Value Equation Scorecard
[table + overall score]

## Top Findings (max 5, ranked by revenue impact)
1. [finding] → [recommended fix] → [agent]

## Quick Wins (ship this week)
## Structural Fixes (need design work)
## Recommended Next Step
[the ONE thing to do first]
```

Keep the audit honest — a 28/30 offer with no traffic is a leadgen problem, say so.
